HOW TO SET UP A NEW PC

1. Unboxing and Initial Setup


1.1 Remove the Packaging Cover.

    1. Carefully cut and remove the outer packaging cover using scissors or a box cutter.

    2. Ensure you don't cut too deeply to avoid damaging the inner box.


    1.2 Remove Edge Boards

    • After removing the packaging cover, gently lift and remove the edge boards from all sides of the box.


    1.3 Remove the Foam Padding

    • Carefully slide out the side pieces of foam, ensuring you support the PC to prevent it from shifting.


    1.4 Lift the PC Out

    1. Cut open the top of the big box but don't lift the heavy PC out, there's an easier way!

    2. Now fold the cardboard box tabs out, we'll turn it over and lift the box off next.

    3. Now the box is open, slowly turn it over.

    4. It's best to turn it over slowly in three stages, up, side then upside down.

    5. Now that the PC box is open side facing down, you can lift off the box easily!

    6. Now that the PC box is open side facing down, you can lift off the box easily!
      • WiFi sticks (if applicable)
      • Documentation and manuals
      • Boxes of small components

    7. Now that the PC box is off, you can remove the polystyrene edge protectors.

    8. then remove the protective bag covering your pc.


    1.5 Unpacking the PC

    1. After removing the protective cover, With the PC lying down with a glass window facing up, remove the glass panel.

    2. To protect the internal components, we have categorised our packaging process into two methods:
      1. For PCs with comparatively smaller size and less weight, we will use packaging foam.
      2. For PCs with larger size and greater weight, we will use RT packs.

    3. Remove the internal protections and proceed to the next step.


    1.6 Check Included Documentation

    • Review the documents and manuals to ensure you have all the necessary information for setup and warranty purposes.


    1.7 Plugging in all the cables

    1. Set up the PC, monitor, and any other required devices on a table.

    2. Plug the power cable into the SMPS socket.

    3. Connect the other end of the power cable to the power supply socket.

    4. Connect the PC and monitor using an HDMI cable
      • If your PC has a GPU then plug in the HDMI in GPU not in the motherboard

    5. Connect the Wi-Fi antenna, if applicable.

    6. Connect keyboard and mouse.

    7. To power on the PC
      • first switch on the power supply.
      • Then, set the SMPS switch from "O" to "I" to turn on the SMPS.
      • Finally, power on the PC.
